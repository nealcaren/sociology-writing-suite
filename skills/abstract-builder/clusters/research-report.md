# Archetype 2: Research-Report

**Prevalence**: 39 abstracts (42.9% of corpus, most common)

## Overview

**Definition**: Abstracts that open by positioning within scholarly conversation (literature, prior research, or direct research statement), then report study findings efficiently.

**Strategic Purpose**: Maximize clarity and efficiency for specialist readers. Position contribution within existing literature. Prioritize content over framing.

**Note**: This archetype includes the former "Gap-Filler" variant, which opens with a gap statement.

## When to Use This Archetype

Use Research-Report when:
- Your audience is specialists in your subfield
- You want to position your study within a scholarly conversation
- You have a clear gap in existing literature to address
- Efficiency and directness are valued
- Your contribution is primarily to existing debates

**Best for**: Specialist audiences, gap-filling contributions, when positioning matters more than phenomenon description.

## Move Sequence Patterns

### Pattern A: Literature-led (most common)
```
S1: PRIOR-RESEARCH
S2: GAP
S3: STUDY-FOCUS
S4: DATA-DESCRIBE
S5: FINDINGS
S6: CLOSE-CONTRIBUTION
```

### Pattern B: Research-led (direct)
```
S1: OPEN-RESEARCH ("This study examines...")
S2: DATA-DESCRIBE
S3: FINDINGS
S4: FINDINGS
S5: CLOSE-CONTRIBUTION
```

### Pattern C: Gap-led variant
```
S1: OPEN-GAP ("Despite X, little is known...")
S2: DATA-DESCRIBE
S3: STUDY-FOCUS
S4: FINDINGS
S5: CLOSE-CONTRIBUTION
```

## Opening Sentence Patterns

### Pattern A: "Scholars/Research [verb] that..."

> "A robust literature has shown that surveillance disproportionately targets poor people of color through the criminal justice and welfare systems."

> "Scholars underline the persistence of gender disparities in the household division of labor."

> "Research demonstrates that diaspora movements play a powerful role in contentious politics and social change in their homelands under a range of conditions."

### Pattern B: "This study [verb] [topic]"

> "This study investigates the social incorporation of unaccompanied, undocumented Latinx youth workers as they come of age in the United States."

> "This study of refugee resettlement contributes a novel conceptual framework to the sociology of forced migration."

### Pattern C: Gap-led variant: "Although/Despite X, [gap]"

> "Although family and migration scholars recognize that intimate partner violence (IPV) can motivate women's movement between countries, little research considers IPV or other gendered family violence further back in women migrants' life histories..."

> "Despite the ubiquitous nature of racist and sexist discourses online, and evidence that racism and sexism are built into the architecture of online spaces, relatively little is known about how people respond to online inequalities."

## Closing Sentence Patterns

### Pattern A: "This research extends/advances..."

> "This research extends our understanding of the reach of surveillance into the lives of the poor even in spaces considered to be private."

> "This research advances our understanding of immigrant youth's lives by examining how institutional context, familial obligations across borders, and limited ethnic networks play a role..."

### Pattern B: "In so doing, [article/study] contributes to..."

> "In so doing, the article contributes to theories of transnationalism, social movements, diaspora politics, and cross-border contentious politics more generally."

### Pattern C: "These findings provide..."

> "These findings provide an understanding of how the transition to legal adulthood affects the way adult children of immigrants help their undocumented parents access resources and information..."

## Transition Phrases

**PRIOR-RESEARCH to GAP**:
- "However, little is known..."
- "Yet, few studies..."
- "However, it remains understudied..."

**GAP to STUDY-FOCUS**:
- "This article addresses this gap by..."
- "Drawing on interviews with..."
- "This study examines..."

**DATA-DESCRIBE patterns**:
- "Drawing on in-depth interviews with [N] [population]..."
- "Based on [N] interviews with..."
- "Using data from..."

## Checklist

Before finalizing a Research-Report abstract, verify:

- [ ] Opening sentence positions in literature OR states study directly
- [ ] Gap is specific (not "more research is needed")
- [ ] Study focus is clear
- [ ] Data description includes N, population, location
- [ ] Findings are concrete and specific
- [ ] Closing extends understanding or contributes to field

## Why This Archetype Works

Research-Report is the most common archetype because:
1. **Clear positioning**: Readers immediately know the scholarly conversation
2. **Efficiency**: Gets to the point without extensive framing
3. **Professional**: Matches reader expectations for specialist journals
4. **Flexible**: Works with literature-led, research-led, or gap-led variants

**Default choice**: When in doubt, use Research-Report. It's safe, professional, and widely accepted.
