# Signature Phrases for Introductions and Conclusions

Common phrases and formulations from sociology interview articles, organized by function.

---

## Introduction Phrases

### Opening Moves

**Phenomenon-led**:
- "[Topic] has become increasingly central to..."
- "[N] percent of [population]..."
- "In recent decades, [trend] has..."
- "[Population] face a distinctive challenge..."
- "The [phenomenon] remains a persistent feature of..."

**Stakes-led**:
- "Few social problems have generated as much..."
- "Understanding [topic] has never been more urgent..."
- "The stakes of [phenomenon] are high..."
- "As [trend] accelerates, scholars and practitioners alike..."
- "[Topic] shapes the life chances of millions..."

**Case-led**:
- "In [location], [event] unfolded..."
- "On [date], [actor] announced..."
- "When [name] arrived at..."
- "Consider the case of [example]..."

**Theory-led**:
- "The concept of [X] refers to..."
- "[Author]'s theory of [X] suggests..."
- "Drawing on [tradition], scholars have shown..."
- "[Framework] offers a lens for understanding..."

### Establishing the Gap

**Gap identification**:
- "Yet we know remarkably little about..."
- "Despite extensive research on [X], scholars have paid less attention to..."
- "Prior research has focused primarily on [X], leaving [Y] unexplored..."
- "What remains unclear is..."
- "Existing studies have not examined..."
- "While [X] is well-documented, [Y] remains understudied..."

**Puzzle framing**:
- "This raises a puzzle: why do..."
- "This pattern is surprising given..."
- "If [expected], why do we observe [actual]?"
- "The persistence of [X] despite [Y] demands explanation..."

**Limitation of prior work**:
- "Previous research has been limited by..."
- "Existing accounts leave unresolved..."
- "While valuable, these studies cannot explain..."
- "The literature has not adequately addressed..."

### Previewing the Study

**Data introduction**:
- "Drawing on [N] in-depth interviews with..."
- "This article examines [topic] through..."
- "Based on [N] semi-structured interviews conducted between..."
- "Using qualitative data from..."
- "To address these questions, we conducted..."

**Argument preview**:
- "We argue that..."
- "This article demonstrates that..."
- "We find that..."
- "Our analysis reveals..."
- "The evidence suggests that..."

**Contribution claims**:
- "This study makes [N] contributions..."
- "We extend [theory] by showing..."
- "We contribute to [literature] by..."
- "The article advances understanding of..."
- "We introduce the concept of [X] to capture..."

### Roadmap Phrases

- "The article proceeds as follows..."
- "We begin by [reviewing/describing]..."
- "We first [X], then [Y], and conclude by [Z]..."
- "In what follows, we..."
- "The remainder of this article..."

## Conclusion Phrases

### Opening Moves

**Restatement**:
- "This study has examined..."
- "[Topic] shapes [population]'s experiences in ways that..."
- "The [phenomenon] we have described..."
- "For [population], [key finding]..."

**Summary**:
- "Three key findings emerge from this analysis..."
- "This article has demonstrated that..."
- "Our analysis reveals that..."
- "The evidence presented here shows..."

**Integration**:
- "Recent research on [topic] has emphasized..."
- "Scholars of [field] have long recognized..."
- "Building on [Author]'s insights..."

### Contribution Claims

**Extending theory**:
- "These findings extend [theory] by showing..."
- "We have demonstrated the utility of [framework] for..."
- "Our analysis confirms and extends [Author]'s argument..."

**Building concepts**:
- "The concept of [X] captures..."
- "We introduced the term [X] to describe..."
- "[Concept] provides analytic leverage for..."
- "Unlike [existing term], [new term] emphasizes..."

**Filling gaps**:
- "This study addresses a gap in..."
- "We have shown that [previously unknown pattern]..."
- "The findings illuminate [understudied phenomenon]..."

**Synthesizing**:
- "By connecting [A] and [B], we reveal..."
- "The integration of [traditions] shows..."
- "Bringing together [literatures], this analysis..."

### Limitations

**Scope limitations**:
- "This study has limitations..."
- "Our sample of [N] [population] in [location] may not generalize to..."
- "We cannot establish causal ordering between..."
- "The [context-specific] nature of our data means..."
- "We make no claim that this is the only way..."

**Methodological limitations**:
- "The cross-sectional nature of our data..."
- "Future research with [different design] could..."
- "Our reliance on [method] means we cannot..."

### Future Directions

- "Future research might examine..."
- "An important next step would be..."
- "Scholars should investigate whether..."
- "Longitudinal research could establish..."
- "Comparative work across [contexts] would..."
- "These findings raise new questions about..."

### Implications

**Theoretical implications**:
- "These findings suggest that theories of [X] should..."
- "For scholars of [field], our analysis implies..."
- "The [mechanism] we identify may operate in..."

**Policy implications**:
- "For practitioners, these findings suggest..."
- "Policymakers should consider..."
- "Current approaches to [X] may inadvertently..."
- "These insights have practical implications for..."

**Broader significance**:
- "More broadly, this study illuminates..."
- "Understanding [phenomenon] becomes increasingly important as..."
- "As [trend] continues, [insight] matters because..."

### Callback and Coda

**Callback phrases**:
- "Returning to [opening theme]..."
- "Recalling our opening observation about..."
- "The [image/case] with which we began..."
- "What we initially characterized as [X] is better understood as..."

**Coda phrases**:
- "Ultimately, [summary statement]..."
- "In the end, [population]'s [experiences] reveal..."
- "As [participant] put it, '[quote]'"
- "What the literature calls [X] may be better understood as..."
- "[Final resonant statement]."

## Phrases to Avoid

### In Introductions
- "This paper argues that..." (too abrupt as opener)
- "In this paper, I will..." (save for roadmap)
- "The literature has overlooked..." (without justification)
- "Little is known about..." (unless truly true)
- "No one has studied..." (probably false)

### In Conclusions
- "In conclusion..." (redundant; you're in the conclusion)
- "To sum up..." (informal)
- "More research is needed" (generic; be specific)
- "This study proves that..." (overclaiming for qualitative work)
- "Future research should..." (weaker than "might examine")

### General
- "It is interesting to note that..." (show, don't tell)
- "Obviously..." / "Clearly..." (if obvious, don't state)
- "The data revealed that..." (passive; prefer active)
- Excessive hedging: "might," "perhaps," "seemingly," "possibly"
